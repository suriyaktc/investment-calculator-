Tool 06

Key Concepts

The essential vocabulary of mutual fund investing, explained plainly with practical examples.

Foundational
NAV — Net Asset Value
The per-unit price of a mutual fund, calculated daily. Total assets minus liabilities, divided by outstanding units. When NAV rises, your investment value grows proportionally.
Fund assets = ₹100 Cr, Units = 1 Cr → NAV = ₹100/unit
The Core Engine
Compounding
Earning returns on your previous returns. The longer you stay invested, the more dramatic the effect — wealth doesn't grow linearly, it accelerates like a snowball rolling downhill.
₹10K at 12% for 30 years = ₹3 Lakh (vs ₹46K via simple interest)
Cost
Expense Ratio
Annual fee charged by the fund as % of assets under management. Deducted daily from NAV. Over decades, even 1% difference compounds significantly against you.
ER 0.1% vs 1.5% on ₹10L over 20 years = ~₹10–15 Lakh less corpus
SIP Benefit
Rupee Cost Averaging
Fixed monthly investment buys more units when prices are low, fewer when high. Over time, your average cost per unit is lower than the average price — automatically, no timing needed.
₹1000/month: NAV ₹50 → 20 units; NAV ₹40 → 25 units. Avg cost: ₹44.44
Tax
LTCG vs STCG
Long-Term Capital Gains (equity held >1 year): taxed at 12.5% above ₹1.25L/year exemption. Short-Term (<1 year): 20%. Holding longer is not just good for compounding — it's tax-smart too.
₹2L LTCG gain: tax only on ₹75K (above exemption) = ₹9,375
Risk Management
Diversification
A mutual fund holds 50–100+ stocks. If one stock crashes 80%, your exposure is typically just 1–2% of the fund. Diversification doesn't eliminate risk — it reduces concentration risk.
1 stock crashes 80%: 2% fund allocation → your portfolio falls only ~1.6%
Performance Metric
CAGR
Compound Annual Growth Rate — the smoothed yearly rate at which an investment grew. The only fair way to compare investments over different time periods. Formula: (End/Start)^(1/years) − 1.
₹1L → ₹3.1L in 10 years → CAGR = (3.1)^0.1 − 1 ≈ 12%
Risk Measures
Standard Deviation & Beta
SD measures volatility — how much returns fluctuate around the average. Beta measures sensitivity to market movement. Beta >1 = more volatile than market. Beta <1 = steadier than market.
Beta 1.3: Market falls 10% → fund likely falls ~13%
Strategy
Step-Up SIP
Increase your SIP amount every year as income grows (typically 5–10%). Even small annual increases dramatically boost your final corpus. Start small — but grow your investment with your salary.
₹5K/month flat for 20yr vs +10%/yr step-up: nearly 2× the corpus
Behavior
Recency Bias
Investors overweight recent performance — chasing last year's top performers or fleeing after a crash. Markets are cyclical. What's best now often won't be best next year. Consistency beats timing.
SIP investors who stayed through 2020 COVID crash recovered fully + more by Dec 2020
Tax Saving
ELSS — 80C Deduction
Equity Linked Savings Scheme. Invest up to ₹1.5L/year to save up to ₹46,800 in taxes (30% slab). 3-year lock-in — shortest of all 80C options. Dual benefit: tax savings + equity returns.
₹1.5L ELSS investment at 30% slab → ₹46,800 immediate tax saving
Planning
Asset Allocation
Dividing investments across asset classes (equity, debt, gold) based on risk profile and horizon. Younger investors can hold more equity. Rebalance annually to maintain target allocation.
Age 30: 80% equity + 20% debt. Age 55: 50% equity + 50% debt (thumb rule)
